# Getting Started with Siriux

## Welcome!

Siriux is your comprehensive solution for project management and team collaboration. This guide will help you get up and running quickly.

## Quick Start (5 Minutes)

### Step 1: Create Your Account

1. **Open your web browser** and go to your Siriux application URL
2. **Click "Sign Up"** in the top right corner
3. **Fill in your information**:
   - **First Name**: Your given name
   - **Last Name**: Your family name
   - **Email**: Your work or personal email
   - **Password**: Create a strong password (8+ characters)
   - **Department**: Your team or department (optional)
4. **Click "Create Account"**

### Step 2: Verify Your Email

1. **Check your email inbox** for a verification message
2. **Click the verification link** in the email
3. **Return to Siriux** and log in with your credentials

### Step 3: Complete Your Profile

1. **Click your profile picture** (top right corner)
2. **Select "Edit Profile"**
3. **Add your information**:
   - **Profile Picture**: Upload a photo of yourself
   - **Phone Number**: For team contact
   - **Bio**: Tell your team about yourself
   - **Time Zone**: For accurate scheduling
4. **Click "Save Profile"**

### Step 4: Create Your First Project

1. **Click "Create Project"** on your dashboard
2. **Fill in project details**:
   - **Project Name**: Give your project a clear name
   - **Description**: Briefly explain what the project is about
   - **Start Date**: When the project begins
   - **End Date**: When you expect to finish
3. **Click "Create Project"**

### Step 5: Add Your First Task

1. **Click "Add Task"** in your new project
2. **Enter task details**:
   - **Task Title**: What needs to be done
   - **Description**: More details about the task
   - **Due Date**: When it should be completed
3. **Click "Create Task"**

**Congratulations!** You're now ready to use Siriux effectively.

## Detailed Setup Guide

### Account Creation

#### Choosing Your Email

**Best Practices:**
- Use an email you check regularly
- Avoid shared email addresses
- Use your work email for professional projects

#### Creating a Strong Password

**Requirements:**
- At least 8 characters long
- Mix of uppercase and lowercase letters
- Include numbers and special characters
- Avoid common words or personal information

**Example Strong Password:** `ProjectManager2024!`

#### Department Selection

Choose the department that best describes your role:
- **Engineering**: Software development, IT, technical teams
- **Marketing**: Marketing, sales, customer success
- **Design**: UX/UI, graphic design, creative teams
- **Operations**: Operations, HR, finance, administration
- **Other**: For specialized or cross-functional roles

### Profile Customization

#### Profile Picture Tips

**Recommended:**
- Professional headshot or clear photo
- Square format works best
- High resolution (at least 400x400 pixels)
- Recent photo that clearly shows your face

**How to Upload:**
1. Click the camera icon on your profile picture
2. Select "Upload Photo"
3. Choose image from your computer
4. Crop and adjust as needed
5. Click "Save"

#### Writing Your Bio

**Keep it concise and helpful:**
- Your role and responsibilities
- Key skills or expertise
- What you're working on
- How you like to collaborate

**Example Bio:**
> "Senior Product Manager with 5 years of experience in SaaS products. Currently leading the Q4 product launch. Love data-driven decisions and cross-functional collaboration."

#### Time Zone Settings

**Why it matters:**
- Accurate deadline calculations
- Proper meeting scheduling
- Team coordination across locations

**How to set:**
1. Go to Profile → Settings
2. Select your time zone from the dropdown
3. Click "Update Settings"

### Project Creation

#### Project Naming

**Good Examples:**
- "Q4 Marketing Campaign Launch"
- "Mobile App Redesign v2.0"
- "Customer Portal Development"

**Avoid:**
- Vague names like "Project X"
- Internal jargon only your team understands
- Names that don't indicate the project's purpose

#### Project Description

**Include:**
- Project goals and objectives
- Key deliverables
- Target audience or users
- Success metrics

**Example Description:**
> "Redesign our mobile application to improve user experience and increase engagement by 25%. Key deliverables include new UI design, improved navigation, and enhanced performance metrics."

#### Project Timeline

**Setting Realistic Dates:**
- **Start Date**: When work actually begins (not when planning starts)
- **End Date**: Include buffer time for unexpected delays
- **Milestones**: Break large projects into smaller phases

**Timeline Tips:**
- Add 10-20% buffer time to your estimates
- Consider team availability and holidays
- Plan for review and feedback cycles

### Task Management

#### Creating Effective Tasks

**Task Title Best Practices:**
- Start with action verbs (Create, Review, Update, Design)
- Be specific and clear
- Keep titles under 50 characters
- Include context if needed

**Good Examples:**
- "Create user login wireframes"
- "Review Q3 marketing metrics"
- "Update homepage copy for launch"

**Poor Examples:**
- "Stuff to do"
- "Marketing things"
- "Fix issues"

#### Task Descriptions

**Include:**
- What exactly needs to be done
- Acceptance criteria (when is it "done"?)
- Relevant context or background
- Dependencies on other tasks

**Example Description:**
> "Design wireframes for the new user login flow. Include screens for: login form, password reset, and social login options. Follow our design system guidelines. Mobile-first approach required."

#### Setting Due Dates

**Smart Due Date Planning:**
- Break large tasks into smaller milestones
- Set realistic deadlines based on complexity
- Consider team member workload
- Build in time for review and revisions

**Due Date Types:**
- **Hard Deadlines**: Cannot be moved (client commitments, launch dates)
- **Soft Deadlines**: Can be adjusted if needed
- **Milestones**: Major project phases

## Team Collaboration

### Inviting Team Members

#### When to Invite Team Members

**Invite Early:**
- Key stakeholders and decision-makers
- Core team members who will work on the project
- Subject matter experts for specific areas

**Invite Later:**
- Occasional contributors
- Stakeholders who only need updates
- External partners or contractors

#### Writing Invitation Messages

**Personalize your message:**
- Explain the project and their role
- Set expectations for involvement
- Provide context about why they're needed
- Include relevant deadlines

**Example Invitation:**
> "Hi Sarah, I'd like to invite you to join our Mobile App Redesign project. Your UX expertise would be valuable for the user research phase we're starting next week. The project runs through December and we'd need about 5-10 hours of your time initially. Let me know if you're available!"

### Team Roles and Permissions

#### Role Types

**Admin:**
- Full access to all project features
- Can invite/remove team members
- Can change project settings
- Can delete the project

**Manager:**
- Can create and manage tasks
- Can invite team members
- Can view all project data
- Cannot delete project or remove admins

**Member:**
- Can view assigned tasks
- Can comment and update task status
- Can upload files
- Limited access to sensitive project data

#### Choosing the Right Role

**Assign Admin to:**
- Project owners
- Team leads
- Primary decision-makers

**Assign Manager to:**
- Department heads
- Senior team members
- Key stakeholders

**Assign Member to:**
- Individual contributors
- Part-time team members
- External consultants

```

├── 01-Planning/

│   └── Meeting Notes/
├── 02-Design/
│   ├── Wireframes/
│   └── Mockups/

│   ├── Code/
```

- Include dates for version control
- Avoid special characters

**Good Examples:**

- `Meeting_Notes_Project_Kickoff_2024-03-10.docx`
- `User_Research_Results_Summary.pdf`

## Communication and Collaboration

### Using @Mentions

**When to Use @Mentions:**
- **@username**: When you need a specific person's input
- **@team**: For announcements to the whole project team
- **@here**: For people currently active in the project

**Best Practices:**
- Be specific about what you need
- Provide context for the mention
- Don't overuse @team for minor issues

**Examples:**
- "@johnsmith: Can you review the latest design mockups?"
- "@team: Quick update - we've completed the user research phase"
- "@here: I'm starting the development sprint now, let me know if you have questions"

### Commenting and Discussions

#### Writing Effective Comments

**Be Clear and Concise:**
- State your main point first
- Provide necessary context
- Use bullet points for lists
- Tag relevant people

**Example Good Comment:**
> "@sarahjohnson: I've reviewed the wireframes (attached). Overall looks great! Two suggestions:
> 1. The login button could be more prominent
> 2. Consider adding a "Remember Me" option
> 
> Let me know when you'd like to discuss these changes."

#### Thread Management

**Keep Discussions Focused:**
- Start new threads for new topics
- Stay on topic within threads
- Use emojis sparingly for professional tone
- Resolve threads when decisions are made

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Login Problems

#### Forgot Password
1. **Click "Forgot Password"** on the login page
2. **Enter your email address**
3. **Check your email** for reset link
4. **Create a new password**
5. **Log in** with your new password

#### Account Locked
- Contact your team admin
- Wait 30 minutes for automatic unlock
- Try resetting your password

#### Browser Issues
- **Clear cache and cookies**
- **Try a different browser**
- **Disable browser extensions**
- **Update your browser**

### Performance Issues

#### Slow Loading
- **Check internet connection**
- **Close other browser tabs**
- **Restart your browser**
- **Try the mobile app**

#### File Upload Problems
- **Check file size limits** (max 100MB per file)
- **Ensure file type is supported**
- **Try a different browser**
- **Compress large files**

### Notification Issues

#### Not Receiving Emails
- **Check spam folder**
- **Add notifications@siriux.com to contacts**
- **Verify email address in profile**
- **Check notification preferences**

#### Mobile Notifications Not Working
- **Enable notifications in device settings**
- **Check app notification permissions**
- **Update the mobile app**
- **Restart your device**
